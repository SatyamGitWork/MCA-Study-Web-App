Unit III Study Guide

Knowledge

The body of facts and principles accumulated by humankind.

  • Declarative: Facts (What)
  • Procedural: How-to (Process)

Ontologies

Formal representation of concepts and relationships used to define and reason about a domain.

Properties of a Good KR System

Representational Adequacy Can represent all necessary knowledge.
Inferential Adequacy Can manipulate structures to derive new knowledge.
Inferential Efficiency Focuses inference in promising directions (optimization).
Acquisitional Efficiency Ability to acquire new info easily.

Decomposes sentences into relationships and entities. More expressive than Propositional Logic.

Constants: john, house-1
Variables: x, y
Predicates: COLOR(house-1, YELLOW)
Functions: father(x)

Quantifiers

  • ∀ Universal: "For all"
  • ∃ Existential: "There exists"

Connectives

∧ AND ∨ OR ¬ NOT → Implies

Conversion Algorithm (8 Steps)

  1. Eliminate Implication: A → B becomes ¬A ∨ B
  2. Reduce Negation: Move ¬ inwards (De Morgan's)
  3. Standardize Variables: Rename for uniqueness
  4. Skolemization: Replace ∃ variables with constants/functions
  5. Prenex Form: Move quantifiers left
  6. CNF: Distribute OR over AND
  7. Separate Clauses: Split conjunctions
  8. Standardize Apart: Rename clause variables

Unification

Finding a substitution (σ) that makes two logical expressions identical.

MGU: Most General Unifier Occurs Check: Prevents loops

Resolution Refutation

Proof by Contradiction:
  1. Negate the Goal.
  2. Convert to Clause Form.
  3. Resolve until NIL (Empty Clause) is found.

Control Strategies

  • Breadth-First: Complete, inefficient.
  • Set of Support: Uses negated goal.
  • Unit Preference: Uses single-literal clauses.
  • Linear Input: Uses base set parents.
