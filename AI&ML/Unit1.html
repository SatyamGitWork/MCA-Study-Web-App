A. Core Definitions

Rich & Knight
Behavior Focus

"Study of how to make computers do things at which people are better at the moment."

Nilsson
Ideas Focus

"Study of ideas that enable computers to be intelligent."

McDermot
Cognition Focus

"Study of mental faculties through the use of computational models."

Buchanan
Knowledge Focus

"Representing knowledge using symbols and heuristic methods."

Scientific Goal

Understand the working of the mind in mechanistic terms. "The mind is what the brain does." - Minsky

Engineering Goal

  • Autonomy: Perform without intervention.
  • Flexibility: Handle variability.
  • Ease of Use: Understand intent.
  • Learning: Improve from experience.

AI is interdisciplinary, built on:

Philosophy

Nature of knowledge, reasoning, & mind.

Mathematics

Logic, computation, probability, optimization.

Psychology

Models of human thought and behavior.

Computer Eng.

Hardware/Software infrastructure.

Linguistics

Language structure for NLP.

Birth of AI

A.M. Turing & Stored Program Computer.

c. 1960

John McCarthy: Created LISP, "Advice Taker". Coined "Artificial Intelligence".

1960s (Optimism)

First chess program. ELIZA (Joseph Weizenbaum) simulated a psychoanalyst.

1970s-80s (Expert Systems)

Knowledge-Based Systems. DENDRAL, MYCIN. First commercial success: R1 ($40M/year saved).

Current State

Machine Learning, Soft Computing, Data Analytics, Generative AI, Agentic AI.

  • Perception: Vision, Speech, Touch.
  • NLP: Understanding, Generation, Translation.
  • Robotics: Sensing & Acting in real world.
  • Planning: Action sequences to reach goals.
  • Expert Systems: Emulate human experts.
  • Core: ML, Theorem Proving, Game Playing.
